High Risk: Grape Powdery Mildew (48% of survey disease reports)

This fungal disease thrives in dry conditions with high humidity. It severely reduces yield and fruit quality.

Recommended Fertilizer Optimization

  • Nitrogen (N): Apply 150 kg/hectare. Avoid excess N as it encourages lush, susceptible growth.
  • Potassium (K): Maintain high levels (250 kg/hectare) to improve plant immunity and fruit set.
  • Foliar Application: Use a 0-52-34 (MKP) spray during the berry development stage to strengthen cell walls.

Disease Control Protocol

  • Preventative: Apply Sulphur powder or a systemic fungicide (e.g., Azoxystrobin) immediately post-pruning.
  • Treatment: If symptoms appear (white, powdery spots), use Dinocap or a Copper Oxychloride spray.
  • Cultural: Improve canopy ventilation and reduce humidity by proper pruning (addressing limited modern techniques).
